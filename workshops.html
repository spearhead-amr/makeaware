Workshops

Hands-on, data-driven workshops are designed to address antimicrobial resistance (AMR) by helping people understand the issue, discuss it together, and take part in collecting and interpreting information. These activities use a grassroots approach to create knowledge, giving participants an active role in understanding how antibiotics are used and how resistance develops. This page presents the workshop activities and documents the protocols, tools, and materials developed throughout the process.

Nine workshop formats have been developed in collaboration with experts in science, communication, and technology to address antimicrobial resistance (AMR) across diverse contexts and audiences. Each format follows an interdisciplinary and participatory approach, encouraging participants to reflect on treatment habits, personal attitudes, and everyday experiences before engaging them in discussion, shared learning, and the co-creation of insights relevant to resistance research.

A variety of tools, such as microscopes, and technologies, including Arduino, are combined with methodologies like data visualisation and physicalisation to foster engagement and raise awareness. By recognising personal habits and experiences as valuable data, participants gain a deeper understanding of their own behaviours, while contributing information that can inform and advance AMR research.
