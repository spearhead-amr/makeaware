Story-collection tool

The story-collection tool is a digital interface for collecting personal stories about antibiotic consumption. Questions structure the narration to guide people in sharing their experiences, serving as prompts that help new relevant aspects emerge and enable a systematic collection of data.

Instructions

If you would like to share your story, please complete the form below by answering the questions and elaborating where you feel it is most relevant based on your experience. If a question does not apply to you or you prefer not to answer, you may leave it blank. We encourage you to include any additional thoughts or details you consider important. You are welcome to write in the language you feel most comfortable using. All data is collected anonymously and stored on the university server. For more information, please refer to the Data Privacy and Management Policy page.

Drawing on narrative medicine, the tool emphasises listening to how people perceive and describe their experiences to highlight what is most important to them while structuring information so meaning and patterns can be identified to inform strategies against antibiotic resistance and foster a shared space for developing more human-centred healthcare solutions. All the shared stories are collected and displayed in the databook.
